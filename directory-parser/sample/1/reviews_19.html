This anime will always have a top spot among GAOAT (Greatest Anime of All Time). Some of the reasons why are clear... a very gripping set of sub plots in each episode that sometimes linger on to the next episode and sometimes are not seen to have lasting effects in the character development. Part of this lack of roll-over into characterization adds a mysteriousness to the tone of anime. This is one of the main reasons why Cowboy Bebop will forever remain in anime watchers' hearts. An amazingly crafted overall tone that intertwines with the plot, leaves strikingly unique characters that in our present day are imitated by cos-players regularly.
The themes in the anime were not that of minor ideologies, but delivered strong themes that could cause drastic changes in people's lives. I couldn't imagine that this anime has not changed many peoples' lives in regards to how they think about life. The creativity for the sub-plots is congruent to an author's dream to write a whole book that would inspire readers. But Cowboy Bebop is able to deliver stories of similar impact in a single episode. All this is done in a futuristic world with old-school animation where the ideas for technologies and methods of doing daily tasks requires true creative genius.
Returning to the reason why Cowboy Bebop is so influential, a single reason cannot actually explain its impact, but a good watch of the anime should be able to do the trick. heh.

Cowboy Bebop is, essentially, an American-style "Western." Set in Space. To an awesome and varied soundtrack, as our anti-heroes bebop around the solar system, from the moon of Saturn to the clouds of Venus, collecting bounties on various criminals, trying to avoid their various troubled and mysterious pasts.

But...

There's a lot of tropes and stereotypes played with masterfully, ironically, humorously, and, strangely enough, straightly, throughout the series, blending East-Meets-West in a delectable fusion not unlike it's outstanding soundtrack.

Blues; Jazz; Rock; Western; Classical; the list goes on-and-on, each "style" represented and counterpointed by its particular episode.

Science Fiction. Comedy. Drama. Action/Adventure. Cowboy Bebop has something for everyone. And, unlike most series that tries to be "everything for everyone," Cowboy Bebop somehow manages to succeed.

Watching Cowboy Bebop is only half the experience; listening is as much a part of the pleasure. Like putting on your headphones and cuing up your favorite playlist of various styles of music, and letting each piece pull your emotions this-way-and-then-that, until at the end, you're satisfied and yet strangely melancholy; you've laughed, you've cried, you've reminisced fondly in turn.

That's Cowboy Bebop.

TL;DR - The episodic nature that involves different genres is a blessing and a curse, but there is some cohesion between each episode. The characters are full and well-developed and the OST is on another level. Additionally, the show is directed beautifully. Although the age has shown in the art, it still looks good. Watch every episode, but if unsure as to whether to watch to completion, try to watch through at least episode 5. English dub is great, too.

--Full Review--

Cowboy Bebop is lauded by many to be the one of the greatest, if not the greatest, piece of art to come from Japan. This praise does lead to a lot of exaggeration of what it does right and the ignorance of the flaws which are in this piece of work. This is not to take away from what Cowboy Bebop does well. When it is good, you might have to take a moment to appreciate it, but then again, what I enjoyed might not be your cup'o'tea.

Cowboy Bebop is episodic in nature, even if the the episodes occur chronologically and a few episodes will recall events of previous episodes. Each episode is a new bounty to collect or a new issue to deal with. There are a few "two part episodes", but that is the longest the show presents a continuous event. On top of this, many of the episodes tackle a different theme or concept that seems to be influenced by a western film. An example of this is "Toys in the Attic", which is inspired by Alien. This, along with the music selection, makes Cowboy Bebop very friendly for newcomers or the western world.

This episodic nature can be a curse and a blessing. While a vast majority of the episodes are extremely well done, there are a few episodes that remain unmemorable. One or two of these are agreed upon, but others are debated largely. This stems from the wide variety of subjects and themes. You might not enjoy "Bohemian Rhapsody" and "Brain Scratch Fever" simply because you are not a fan of the thriller, mystery genre, but highly enjoy the comedic nature of "Cowboy Funk" and "Mushroom Samba" instead. The combination of styles and genres leads to biases from the audience, but at the same time guarantees that there is something for everyone to love.

However, for a first time watch through, watching every episode is a good idea. They play off of each other very well, and this is part of the reason this show is so highly praised. Each episode fleshes out the crew of the Bebop even more, sometimes causing you to recall previous episodes to see how the character has either changed or remained the same.

Additionally, for a first time watcher, I recommend trying to stick it out until you have completed episode 5 "Ballad of the Fallen Angels". I have yet to read or hear that someone disliked that episode. The characterization, the action, the music, etc. are all present and done well. I have heard other people say that this is where the show really starts. I do tend to agree, but at the same time, the four episodes previous are all necessary to get the most out of it.

Also, the English dub is really well done. Steve Blum as Spike alone makes the English a recommended watch, but all of the other characters are cast with great choices. I have yet to watch the Japanese except through clips, but I have watched it in English multiple times. I'll stick to English on this one. I tend to not have preference in the matter when picking a show to watch, but this one has my vote for English 100% of the time.

Now that I got that all out of the way, I'll try to hit the different aspects of the show.

--Story--(8)

The episodic nature does prevent a true over-arching story to unfold through the show, but each episode carries its own unique personality. All of the episodes are well done, but at the same time, there are forgettable episodes. The backstory, however, is well presented for each character, leading up to one of the best endings for a show that I've ever seen.

--Art--(7)

For the age of the show, the artwork has held up. It might not be as clean as modern shows, or as fluid, but it still can be breathtaking. The age shows a bit, but how its held up over the years and the use of shots to help tell the story that is being portrayed is a testament to the show itself.

--Sound--(10)

That the OST for this show is great is practically indisputable. The use of Jazz, Rock, Chorus, and more leads to a beautiful mix of sounds. The OP and ED are also amazing, making it difficult to skip if you tend to do that. Music is also used well throughout the show. It is placed in great moments and really helps get any message across.

--Enjoyment--(9)

This is something that will vary most among people who have watched this show. Certain episodes will appeal more than others. This is something that is unavoidable, however, the show can still be captivating, even during those episodes that you might think that you would be inclined to skip.

Cowboy Bebop is a quite simplistic series. There's an overarching plot in there, but mostly it's an episodic series that starts the first couple of episodes setting up some plot points, spending an episode in the middle bringing it up again (so you don't forget, I guess) and in the final episode it all comes to a close. The rest of the episodes are basically you following the crew on their adventures and getting to know them a bit more.

Normally, this would be a problem for me. This series, albeit short with only 26 episodes, is like Naruto on steroids. If you decided to skip the episodes that have nothing to do with the set up plot, you would have an anime of about six episodes. However, it's the simplicity of Cowboy Bebop that makes this somehow work. It's not 500 episodes long, which makes me... not mind it at all, so to speak. In fact, it does the job like a charm.

The cast is nice and compact, with only four characters to focus on, which the series does a good job of fleshing out. At the end of the series, I found myself not really having a favourite character, in the sense that every character was nicely balanced out and all just as likable as the other.

My biggest praise goes to the sound design. This series does sound REALLY well. It's a pretty quiet series, featuring sound effects and voice acting mostly. Only on selected occasions are you treated by the series' outstanding music.

In the 23 years long life I've led until now, I never was very big on anime. Sure, I grew up with Pokemon, but that was really about it. Really, this is like my first serious anime. My indifference towards anime completely changed after having finished Cowboy Bebop. It's the first anime I watched from start to end, in about a week or so, and I'm planning to watch many more anime in the future.

Cowboy Bebop is amazing in all its simplicity. It's a mature tale, with nothing too grand and epic. Definitely would watch again.

Overall 10

Cowboy Bebop was, what you might call, my "gateway anime". Basically, it was the first anime I ever watched all of the way through. Sure, when I was younger I saw some episodes of Pokemon here and there, but I never actually started and completed an anime series in full. So, therefore, Cowboy Bebop was my first. And, while I may be a bit biased because of that, I think I can safely say that Cowboy Bebop is a masterpiece, or as close to a masterpiece as it can get.

STORY: 9/10
Without delving in spoilers, Cowboy Bebop follows a group of bounty hunters on the Bebop, an old and worn down spaceship. Almost every episode follows a new adventure, almost completely unrelated to the previous. In one episode, they may be targeting a drug dealer in the deserts of the Tijuana asteroid colony. The next, they may be on Mars, targeting a dog thief. As you may gather from this, the series is very episodic, with only a few episodes revolving around the "underlying story". I quote "underlying story", because when you're watching Cowboy Bebop, you're not watching it for an epic science fiction noir story that spans multiple episodes. You're watching it for the here and now. And while, yes, the "underlying story" is interesting, and you want to see it play out, that's never the main focus. The focus is on the character interactions and the "bounty of the week" format. And, for what it is, it's great.

If I were to have any complaints about the story, it would be that not every episode is as great as others. For example, the episode "Boogie Woogie Feng Shui" is not nearly as good as the episode before it, "Pierrot le Fou", or the episode after it, "Cowboy Funk". And while, yes, having every episode be amazing is a hard feat, when this show is great, it is really great. But, you can definitely tell that some episodes, just aren't as good as others. But, in spite of this, I still give a 9 to the story, because for me, hits like "Ballad of Fallen Angels" overshadow episodes like "Heavy Metal Queen".

ART: 9/10
Plenty of anime series from the 90s have a distinct look and feel to them. A lot of the time, this look and feel is dated, and doesn't hold up quite as well today. An example of this, would Trigun, a very similar anime to Cowboy Bebop. Cowboy Bebop, on the other hand, doesn't have this problem, and most of the art and animation holds up well, even 20 years later. Watching this, it's sometimes hard to believe it's from 1998, and it makes you wonder what they could've done had they had today's technology and equipment. The animation is incredibly fluid, a perfect example of this being the fight scene in "Asteroid Blues". The art is beautiful, a perfect example of this being the church scene in "Ballad of Fallen Angels". Again, if I were to have any complaints with the art, it would be that the digitally rendered scenes don't hold up quite as well as the hand drawn sequences. But, this scenes are few and far between, and aren't horrible to look at, so I still give the art a 9 out of 10.

SOUND: 10/10
Perhaps the category Cowboy Bebop is most well known for, the sound is outstanding in most every way. The music is amazing, and fits quite nicely with every scene it's in. While Cowboy Bebop is known for having a jazz and blues soundtrack, it's surprising just how varied it really is. For example, "Heavy Metal Queen" has a very, well, heavy metal inspired soundtrack. "Ballad of Fallen Angels" has a very lyrical soundtrack, with songs such as "Rain" and "Green Bird". And, of course, it would be a disservice not to mention the amazing OP and ED sequences, with "Tank!" and "The Real Folk Blues" respectively. Both of these songs are now iconic, and immediately identifiable as Cowboy Bebop. Overall, the OST is one of the best in anime history.

Another aspect of the sound I wanted to touch on, is the dub. While the sub is very good, and I have zero complaints with it, it says something when even the original director of the anime says the English dub is better. This is the one anime where almost every one agrees that the dub is better. Even avid sub watchers, who almost always refuse to watch anything else, agree that the dub is better for Cowboy Bebop. Every actor gave it their all, and every voice fits the character. There's never a time when the dub doesn't match up with the lip movements. And, of course, the dub features the masterful work from Steve Blum, as Spike Spiegel. There's no wonder this guy is considered one of the best in the business. His work as Spike is consistently amazing, and possibly some of the best voice work in any animation, be it anime or not. Overall, the sound definitely deserves a 10/10 in my opinion. I have zero complaints, not even nitpicks.

CHARACTERS: 10/10
This is another category where Cowboy Bebop really shines. The chemistry and the interactions between each of the main characters, is one of the draws of the show in my opinion. It's what each episode relies on, and why it's enjoyable to keep coming back to the show, even when each episode is very episodic. Each crew member of the Bebop has their own complex personality, and their own complex problems from their past. I don't have a whole lot else to say when it comes to the characters, without just talking about each character and delving into spoilers. It's just something that's best figured out while watching. Overall though, the characters are perfect, and I give this category a 10/10.

ENJOYMENT: 10/10
When it comes to my personal enjoyment of the show, I would have to give this category a 10. Like I said at the beginning, this was my first anime, and it was a great one to start out with. Almost everything about it caught my interest, and I ended up finishing it within a week. It opened my eyes to a whole other medium of entertainment, that was unlike anything I'd ever seen. Cowboy Bebop is still one of the best anime I've ever seen, and for that, I would have to give the anime the following rating:

OVERALL: 10/10
